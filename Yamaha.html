Yamaha YZF‑R1

Born from MotoGP: A 998cc CP4 Crossplane Engine That Delivers Seamless Torque and Razor-Sharp Control.

The Yamaha YZF‑R1 is powered by a 998cc inline four-cylinder CP4 engine with a crossplane crankshaft, delivering strong, linear torque. It features titanium connecting rods and intake valves, forged aluminum pistons, and a variable intake system (YCC‑I) for optimized performance at different RPMs. The six-speed gearbox includes an assist and slipper clutch to ensure smooth shifting under hard acceleration and braking.

This bike comes equipped with a 6-axis Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) that powers advanced rider aids such as lean-sensitive traction control, slide control, lift control, and launch control. Riders can switch between various power modes to match road or track conditions. A Quick Shift System enables clutchless gear changes, and Yamaha’s Communication Control Unit allows for GPS data logging and performance analysis.

The YZF‑R1 features a Deltabox aluminum frame for optimal rigidity and balance. It includes fully adjustable KYB suspension for precise handling, and high-performance Brembo Stylema front brake calipers paired with a radial master cylinder for superior braking control.

Inspired by Yamaha’s MotoGP machines, the R1’s aerodynamic bodywork includes integrated winglets that generate downforce at speed. The sleek fairings also contribute to airflow efficiency and engine cooling.

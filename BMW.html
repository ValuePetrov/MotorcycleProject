BMW M 1000 RR

Track-Bred Precision: 999cc ShiftCam Engine Tuned for 205 HP and Pure Racing DNA.

The BMW M 1000 RR is powered by a 999cc inline-four engine that uses BMW’s ShiftCam technology to vary valve timing and lift. It delivers 205 horsepower at 13,000 rpm, with peak performance optimized for racing. The engine is redlined at 15,100 rpm and includes titanium valves and lightweight forged pistons for maximum responsiveness.

This motorcycle features advanced electronics including Dynamic Traction Control (DTC), wheelie control, launch control, and engine brake control. Multiple ride modes allow customization for road or track conditions. The Competition package adds an M GPS lap trigger and race telemetry options.

The chassis is built around a lightweight aluminum bridge frame. It features Öhlins suspension front and rear, with Full Floater Pro linkage for precise rear-wheel control. High-spec CNC-machined parts add strength and reduce weight.

The M 1000 RR is fitted with carbon fiber winglets and aerodynamic fairings designed in a wind tunnel. These components increase downforce and enhance stability, especially at speeds exceeding 300 km/h. Integrated brake cooling ducts further optimize race-day performance.
